ISGS-Reason: Instance-Semantic Embedded 3D Gaussian Splatting and Novel View Reasoning for Comprehensive Scene Understanding

Xihan Wang, Tianji Jiang, Yu Gao, Jiadong Tang
Beijing Institute of Technology
TMM
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Abstract

Scene understanding integrates semantic and spatial representations within 3D environments. With the advent of 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS), a new paradigm for 3D scene representation has emerged, providing continuous novel-view rendering while maintaining fine-grained visual fidelity. Despite these advantages, existing 3DGS-based scene understanding pipelines still exhibit three critical limitations: inaccurate semantic features due to feature compression and alpha-blending based training; absence of spatial consistency constraints leading to imprecise 3D segmentation; and limited spatial reasoning capability for 3D querying. To address these challenges, we propose ISGS-Reason, a novel framework that integrates Instanceâ€“Semantic Embedded 3D Gaussian Splatting with Novel View Reasoning for comprehensive scene understanding. ISGS-Reason embeds instance and semantic features via direct feature lifting without compression or alpha-blending based training. Furthermore, an Instance-Consistency Feature Refinement (ICFR) module is introduced to enforce intra-instance smoothness and inter-instance distinctness. Moreover, ISGS-Reason achieves spatial reasoning through a query-guided spherical view sampling and chain-of-thought inference mechanism. Extensive experiments on multiple benchmarks demonstrate that ISGS-Reason outperforms state-of-the-art 3DGS-based scene understanding approaches, providing a unified solution for 3D segmentation and visual grounding.
